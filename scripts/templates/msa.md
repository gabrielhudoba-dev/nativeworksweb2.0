RECITALS

Native Works provides design, product, and digital services. Client wishes to engage Native Works for such services on an ongoing basis. The Parties agree that this MSA shall govern all current and future Statements of Work entered into by the Parties, unless a Statement of Work expressly overrides a named provision of this MSA.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

1.1  "Background IP" means all intellectual property owned or licensed by either Party prior to the Effective Date, or developed independently of any Statement of Work.

1.2  "Change Order" means a written document amending an in-progress Statement of Work to reflect additions, removals, or modifications to scope, timeline, or fees.

1.3  "Confidential Information" has the meaning given in Article 7.

1.4  "Deliverable" means any work product, design, document, code, or other output specified in a Statement of Work.

1.5  "Fees" means the amounts payable by Client to Native Works as set out in a Statement of Work.

1.6  "Statement of Work" or "SOW" means a project-specific agreement referencing this MSA, executed by both Parties, describing Deliverables, timeline, revision rounds, acceptance criteria, and Fees.

1.7  "Work Product" means Deliverables created specifically for Client pursuant to a Statement of Work, excluding Background IP.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

ARTICLE 2 — STATEMENTS OF WORK AND ORDER OF PRECEDENCE

2.1  Each SOW is incorporated into and governed by this MSA. A SOW may override a specific, named provision of this MSA only if it expressly states: "This SOW overrides Section [X] of the MSA." All other MSA provisions remain in full force.

2.2  If there is any conflict between this MSA and a SOW that is not resolved by Section 2.1, this MSA shall prevail.

2.3  Native Works may begin work under a SOW only upon receipt of written Client approval (email is sufficient) and, where applicable, the deposit specified in the SOW.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

ARTICLE 3 — FEES, PAYMENT, AND EXPENSES

3.1  Client shall pay Fees in accordance with the payment schedule in each SOW. Unless a SOW states otherwise, invoices are due within fourteen (14) calendar days of the invoice date.

3.2  All Fees are exclusive of VAT. VAT shall be added at the applicable Slovak rate at the time of invoicing.

3.3  Overdue amounts shall accrue statutory default interest under Act No. 513/1991 Coll. (Commercial Code) from the due date until paid in full. Native Works reserves the right to suspend all active work without liability until all overdue amounts are settled.

3.4  Reasonable out-of-pocket expenses pre-approved in writing by Client (including third-party licences, stock imagery, travel) shall be invoiced at cost, with no mark-up, and are due under the same payment terms as Fees.

3.5  Native Works shall issue a final invoice upon completion of each SOW milestone or delivery of final Deliverables, as applicable.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

ARTICLE 4 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1  Assignment. Upon receipt of full and final payment of all Fees due under a SOW, Native Works assigns to Client all copyright and other intellectual property rights in the Work Product created under that SOW, to the fullest extent permitted by applicable law.

4.2  Retention of Background IP. Native Works retains all rights in Background IP, including tools, frameworks, libraries, methodologies, design systems, and know-how. No Background IP is transferred to Client.

4.3  Background IP Licence. To the extent Background IP is incorporated into any Deliverable, Native Works grants Client a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free, perpetual licence to use that Background IP solely to the extent necessary to use and benefit from the Deliverable.

4.4  Rights Held Pending Payment. Until all Fees under a SOW are paid in full, Native Works retains all intellectual property rights in all Deliverables. Client may not use, distribute, or publish any Deliverable before payment is complete.

4.5  Pre-Existing Client Materials. Client retains all rights in any materials, data, or content provided by Client for incorporation into Deliverables. Client warrants that such materials do not infringe any third-party rights.

4.6  Third-Party Components. Native Works shall disclose any third-party components (open-source libraries, licensed stock assets, fonts) incorporated into Deliverables. Client acknowledges that Client's use of such components may be subject to third-party licence terms, and Client is responsible for maintaining any required licences post-delivery.

4.7  Portfolio Rights. Native Works may display completed Deliverables in its portfolio and reference Client as a customer in marketing materials. Client may request confidentiality treatment in writing within thirty (30) days of final delivery, and Native Works shall comply.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

ARTICLE 5 — REVISIONS, ACCEPTANCE, AND CHANGE ORDERS

5.1  Each SOW shall specify the number of revision rounds included in the Fees. Additional revisions beyond the included rounds shall be billed at Native Works' then-current hourly rate, as quoted in advance.

5.2  Revision means a modification that remains within the original project brief. A request that alters the scope, objectives, or requirements of the project constitutes a scope change, which requires a Change Order.

5.3  Following delivery of any Deliverable, Client has five (5) business days to provide written feedback. Silence after this period constitutes deemed acceptance. Feedback must be specific and tied to the original brief; general dissatisfaction without reference to agreed criteria does not constitute grounds for rejection.

5.4  Change Orders must be proposed in writing by either Party. A Change Order becomes binding only when signed (or approved via email) by both Parties. All work completed prior to an approved Change Order is deemed accepted.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

ARTICLE 6 — REPRESENTATIONS, WARRANTIES, AND DISCLAIMERS

6.1  Each Party represents and warrants that: (a) it is duly organised and authorised to enter into this Agreement; (b) this Agreement does not conflict with any other obligation to which it is bound; (c) the person executing this Agreement is duly authorised to do so.

6.2  Native Works warrants that: (a) services will be performed with reasonable skill, care, and professionalism consistent with industry standards; (b) Native Works has secured appropriate licences for any third-party materials incorporated into Deliverables, to the extent disclosed.

6.3  Client warrants that: (a) materials provided to Native Works do not infringe third-party intellectual property rights; (b) Client has all rights necessary to authorise Native Works to use such materials.

6.4  EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET OUT IN THIS MSA OR A SOW, NEITHER PARTY MAKES ANY OTHER WARRANTY, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE. NATIVE WORKS DOES NOT WARRANT THAT DELIVERABLES WILL ACHIEVE ANY SPECIFIC BUSINESS OUTCOME.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

ARTICLE 7 — CONFIDENTIALITY

7.1  "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other in connection with the services that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood as confidential.

7.2  Each Receiving Party shall: (a) hold the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) use it solely for purposes of this Agreement; (c) disclose it only to employees, contractors, or advisors with a need to know and bound by equivalent obligations.

7.3  Exclusions mirror those in any NDA executed between the Parties. If a separate NDA exists, the NDA governs pre-contract disclosures; this Article governs disclosures under and in connection with active SOWs.

7.4  Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for three (3) years, and indefinitely for trade secrets.

ARTICLE 8 — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

8.1  NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF REVENUE, PROFITS, DATA, OR GOODWILL, ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

8.2  NATIVE WORKS' TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY TO CLIENT ARISING OUT OF OR IN CONNECTION WITH THIS AGREEMENT AND ALL SOWs SHALL NOT EXCEED THE GREATER OF: (A) THE TOTAL FEES PAID BY CLIENT TO NATIVE WORKS IN THE THREE (3) MONTHS IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM; OR (B) €5,000.

8.3  The limitations in this Article shall not apply to: (a) liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence; (b) liability for fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation; (c) any other liability that cannot be limited by applicable law.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

ARTICLE 9 — TERM AND TERMINATION

9.1  This MSA commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated by either Party upon sixty (60) days' written notice, provided all active SOWs have concluded or are otherwise terminated.

9.2  Either Party may terminate this MSA or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within fourteen (14) days of written notice; (b) becomes insolvent, is placed into liquidation, receivership, or administration, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors.

9.3  Upon termination of a SOW: (a) Client shall pay all Fees for work completed and expenses incurred to the date of termination; (b) if Client terminates for convenience after work has commenced under a SOW, a kill fee equal to thirty percent (30%) of the remaining unpaid Fees under that SOW shall be immediately due and payable; (c) Native Works shall deliver all completed Deliverables to Client upon receipt of payment.

9.4  The following Articles survive any termination or expiration: 4 (IP), 7 (Confidentiality), 8 (Limitation of Liability), and 10 (General).

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

ARTICLE 10 — GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1  Independent Contractors. The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates any employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.

10.2  Non-Solicitation. During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months thereafter, neither Party shall directly solicit or hire any employee or contractor of the other Party who was involved in the performance of services, without the prior written consent of the other Party.

10.3  Force Majeure. Neither Party shall be liable for delay or failure to perform caused by circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including acts of God, war, government actions, pandemics, or internet infrastructure failures, provided the affected Party gives prompt notice and uses reasonable efforts to mitigate.

10.4  Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Slovak Republic. The Parties exclude the application of the United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods.

10.5  Dispute Resolution. The Parties shall attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith executive-level negotiation for thirty (30) days before initiating legal proceedings. Disputes not resolved through negotiation shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the competent courts of the Slovak Republic.

10.6  Entire Agreement. This MSA, together with all SOWs and any DPA or NDA executed between the Parties, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding its subject matter and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements.

10.7  Amendment. Any amendment to this MSA must be in writing, signed by authorised representatives of both Parties, and expressly identified as an amendment to this MSA.

10.8  Waiver. No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right shall constitute a waiver of that right.

10.9  Severability. If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, that provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable, and the remainder of the Agreement continues in effect.

10.10  Notices. All notices shall be in writing and delivered by email with read receipt or return acknowledgement to the contacts specified in the most recent SOW or as otherwise notified in writing.

10.11  Assignment. Neither Party may assign this Agreement or any SOW without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that Native Works may assign to an affiliate or in connection with a merger or acquisition of its business.
