DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into as of {{date}} and is incorporated into the Master Services Agreement between Native Works s.r.o. and {{legal_name}} of the same date ("MSA"). Capitalised terms not defined here have the meaning given in the MSA.

This DPA satisfies the requirements of Article 28 of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR") and Act No. 18/2018 Coll. on Personal Data Protection (Slovak Republic).

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. ROLES AND CONTEXT

1.1  For the purposes of personal data processed by Native Works on Client's behalf in connection with the services described in Statements of Work (e.g., operating Client's website analytics, sending communications from Client's platforms, processing Client's customer lists), the Parties agree that:
     (a) Client is the Data Controller;
     (b) Native Works is the Data Processor.

1.2  Native Works' processing of personal data on its own behalf (e.g., for its own accounting, CRM, or supplier management) is governed by Native Works' own privacy policy and is not subject to this DPA.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

2. SUBJECT MATTER, NATURE, AND PURPOSE OF PROCESSING

2.1  Subject matter: Personal data of Client's customers, users, or employees processed by Native Works in the course of providing the services.

2.2  Duration: For the term of the MSA and each applicable SOW, plus any statutory retention period required by law.

2.3  Nature and purpose: Design, development, testing, content management, analytics configuration, email platform management, and related technical services as specified in SOWs, for the purpose of Client's internal business operations.

2.4  Types of personal data processed may include: names, email addresses, usage data, cookies and identifiers, IP addresses, and such other data as is reasonably required to perform the services.

2.5  Categories of data subjects: Client's customers, website visitors, employees, and other individuals whose data is contained in systems to which Native Works is granted access under a SOW.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

3. OBLIGATIONS OF NATIVE WORKS AS PROCESSOR

Native Works agrees to:

3.1  Process personal data only on documented instructions from Client, including with regard to transfers outside the EEA, unless required by Union or Slovak law (in which case Native Works shall inform Client of that legal requirement before processing, to the extent permitted by law).

3.2  Ensure that persons authorised to process the personal data have committed to confidentiality obligations at least equivalent to those in this DPA.

3.3  Implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures pursuant to Article 32 GDPR, including at minimum: encryption of data in transit and at rest; access controls and principle of least privilege; logging and monitoring of access; and documented incident response procedures.

3.4  Not engage any sub-processor without: (a) prior specific or general written authorisation from Client; and (b) imposing on that sub-processor data protection obligations equivalent to those set out in this DPA by way of a written agreement. Client grants general authorisation to engage the sub-processors listed in Schedule A, with thirty (30) days' prior notice required for any changes to Schedule A.

3.5  Assist Client in responding to requests from data subjects exercising their rights under Chapter III of the GDPR (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection), taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available to Native Works.

3.6  Assist Client in ensuring compliance with Articles 32–36 GDPR (security, breach notification, DPIA, prior consultation), taking into account the nature of processing and the information available to Native Works.

3.7  At Client's choice, delete or return all personal data to Client upon termination of the services to which this DPA relates, and delete existing copies, unless Union or Slovak law requires continued storage (in which case Native Works shall notify Client of such obligation).

3.8  Make available to Client all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and allow for and contribute to audits and inspections conducted by Client or Client's mandated auditor, provided that: (a) Client gives at least thirty (30) days' advance written notice; (b) audits occur no more than once per year unless required by a supervisory authority; (c) Client bears all costs of such audits.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

4. PERSONAL DATA BREACHES

4.1  Native Works shall notify Client without undue delay, and in any event within forty-eight (48) hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting personal data processed under this DPA. Notification shall include, to the extent known: (a) nature of the breach and categories of data affected; (b) estimated number of data subjects affected; (c) likely consequences; (d) measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

4.2  Native Works shall document all personal data breaches and the measures taken in response.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

5. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

5.1  Native Works shall not transfer personal data outside the EEA unless: (a) the transfer is to a country for which the European Commission has adopted an adequacy decision; (b) appropriate safeguards are in place (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses); or (c) Client has given explicit written consent.

5.2  Where transfers rely on Standard Contractual Clauses, Native Works shall maintain and provide copies upon request.

7. GENERAL

7.1  This DPA is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Slovak Republic and GDPR.

7.2  In the event of any conflict between this DPA and the MSA on matters of data protection, this DPA shall prevail.

7.3  Any amendment to this DPA must be in writing and signed by both Parties.
